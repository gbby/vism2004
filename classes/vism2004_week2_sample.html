VISM2004 Week 2 Journal!!

Continuing your journal, this week you are asked to respond to one of the following two prompts:

  • Imagine that you are a researcher at SRI in the early 1970s. You have classified information that needs to be shared with a research team at MIT. Taking into account the differences between centralized and distributed (packet-switched) network topologies, write a brief speculative scenario describing how your message might cross the country. Be creative in imagining variables that could influence your narrative, and feel free to include images.
    This short piece from the Week 1 supplementary readings might be helpful: http://www.rand.org/about/history/baran.html
    If you would like to use images in your .html and don't know how, consult this page: http://www.w3schools.com/html/html_images.asp
  • Abbate writes: "The qualities valued by the original, nonprofit ARPANET community remain embedded in today's commercial Internet: in particular, the emphasis on flexibility and openness in the research community led to a more open, adaptable, and universally usable system. Building on this technical foundation, the business sector helped to broaden access to network products and services, provide user-friendly interfaces, and offer content that would appeal to the general public."
    Given our discussion about the original "purpose" of the internet, ruminate on how these embedded values might influence your personal browsing experience. A few considerations that might guide your writing: do you favour open sharing, content consumption, or both? Do you think the internet is a public good, like water, requiring public development and management, or is it something best left to the corporate sector to develop, package, and sell? These do not have to be mutually exclusive claims.

Remember, this is not an "intro to web coding" exercise, so do not stress if you can't figure out how to write this in .html. For now, just do your best.
