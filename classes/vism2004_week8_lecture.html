Convergence Culture
"...no one is a passive consumer anymore because everyone is a media outlet."
- Clay Shirkey

Characteristics

Convergence is a cultural rather than a technological process


In a networked society, people increasingly form knowledge communities


New forms of participatory culture


We acquire skills through our recreational lives we can later apply to more serious pursuits


Convergence culture brings with it a reconceptualization of the audience

Free Labour?

The question Of labor in a "digital economy" is not so easily dismissed as an innovative development of the familiar logic of capitalist exploitation


The conditions that make free labor an important element of the digital economy are based in a difficult, experimental compromise between the historically rooted cultural and affective desire for creative production and the current capitalist emphasis on knowledge as the main source of value-added

"Digital economy" as a specific mechanism of internal "capture" of larger pools of social and cultural knowledge


The collective nature of networked, immaterial labor has been simplified by the utopian statements of the cyberlibertarians


Labour is not equivalent to waged labour


The internet is about the extraction of value out of continuous, updateable work


Free labour is structural to the late capitalist cultural economy

Dear Moshev: Intimate? Instagram is advertising. There's nothing intimate about advertising.
