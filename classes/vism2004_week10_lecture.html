Far too often, the balancing of privacy interests against security interests takes place in a manner that severely shortchanges the privacy interest while inflating the security interests.

- Daniel Solove
As our lives become mediated by the convenience of digital technology, information about them becomes subject to commercial capture: a logic that I describe as a form of ‘digital enclosure’ of personal information.

- Mark Andrejevic

The Digital Enclosure

A key legal issue in coming years will be the control and use of information gathered about consumers and citizens as they go about their wired lives.


Not just what information companies will collect about us, but how this information will be put to use.


The rapid growth of digital media is predicated upon an emerging surveillance-based commercial model.


Any attempt to locate privacy rights at the level of individual consumption choices reflects the power relations that structure those choices.

Digital Enclosure (Cont.)

‘Digital enclosure’ – the creation of an interactive realm wherein every action, interaction, and transaction generates information about itself.


Much of the celebratory hype over the way in which the internet creates a new generation of audience-producers blurs the important line between access to the means of online content production and ownership or control over these resources.

Surveillance is a distinctive product of the modern world. Indeed, surveillance helps to constitute the world as modern.

- David Lyon

Surveillance

Technological developments and social processes mutually influence and shape/co-construct each other.


Dataveillance: the systematic monitoring of people's actions or communications through the application of information technology.


Not only does surveillance now extend beyond the administrative reach of the nation-state into corporate and especially consumer capitalist spheres, it also extends geographically.

Surveillance (cont.)

Enabled by new technologies, surveillance at the start of the new century is networked, polycentric, and multidimensional, including biometric and video techniques as well as more conventional dataveillance.


With the proliferation of surveillance at all levels, enabled by new technologies, the very notion of a fixed identity, to which records correspond, has become more dubious.

Surveillance (cont.)

An examination of surveillance practices underscores the ways in which modes of control and influence are connected with deeper determinants of life chances and of social ordering. Such practices are the nexus of co-construction, where technological potential meets social pressure.

The modern response is classically consistent; namely, to argue that concern for privacy is an antidote to such technicized surveillance, so that the sacrosanct self can be sheltered behind legal limits on personal data collection. This neatly answers the problem as perceived by the subjects of the data collection and thus should be treated seriously. It also resonates with deep philosophical commitments, for example, to the self as communicative and as possessing inherent dignity.

- David Lyon
